Standard Room
Single or double bed, basic furnishings, and essential amenities like air conditioning, TV, and Wi-Fi.

This is the most basic type of room in a hotel. It usually includes standard amenities such as a bed, bathroom, television, and a small work area. It's designed for guests who want a comfortable stay without luxury extras.

Superior Room
Larger space, upgraded bedding, enhanced decor, and amenities like a minibar and higher-end toiletries.

A step up from the standard room, superior rooms are often larger and feature better decor, more comfortable bedding, and sometimes better views. They may also come with additional amenities like a mini-fridge or a coffee maker.

Deluxe Room
Larger size, premium bedding, larger bathrooms with better fixtures, possibly with a balcony or a better view.

Deluxe rooms offer more space, better furnishings, and additional perks. These rooms are typically more stylish and comfortable, often with a better view of the surroundings.

Suite
Separate living area, premium furniture, often includes a kitchenette or dining space, and additional luxury features like jacuzzis or multiple bathrooms.

A suite is a large, luxurious room with multiple areas, often including a living room, bedroom, and sometimes a kitchen or dining area. This room type is perfect for guests seeking more privacy and space during their stay.

Junior Suite
Larger than a standard room, separate sitting area, and higher-end amenities compared to a standard room.

A junior suite is smaller than a full suite but still offers more space than a standard room. It typically consists of one main room that combines the living and sleeping areas, along with a larger bathroom and better furnishings.
